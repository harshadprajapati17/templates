Scroll ideas gallery

Ten quiet GSAP patterns built on controlled scroll physics.

Each block below is an isolated recipe—designed to be read, reused, and extended without disturbing the main narrative above.

Idea 01 · Scrubbed list

Basic fade and rise on scroll

A column of lines that quietly lifts into place as you move through the section.

  • Anchor a simple scrubbed timeline to one section.
  • Let opacity and elevation carry most of the feeling.
  • Use it as a base pattern for richer sequences.

Idea 02 · Staggered cards

Cards that step into view

Three simple tiles that arrive in sequence, tuned to scroll instead of time.

First signal

Lead with the primary card and let it anchor the eye.

Supporting layer

Follow with context that feels deliberately delayed.

Final note

Close with a quiet, optional piece of detail.

Progress through the copy.

Idea 03 · Vertical progress

A rail that keeps pace with you

As you read, a thin column quietly tracks how far you have moved through the thoughts.

Define the lane

Start with a fixed-height rail that can accept a fill element.

Attach to scroll

Map the rail fill to the section height instead of the page.

Taper the motion

Keep ease neutral so the progress feels informational, not dramatic.

Idea 04 · Parallax text

Foreground and background move at different speeds

A slow, confident offset between layers builds depth without relying on heavy imagery.

Idea 05 · Pinned copy

One calm message, held in place

The left side stays with you while the right quietly cycles through supporting details.

Anchor the headline

Keep the primary thought pinned while secondary lines move.

Calibrate duration

Let the pin release exactly when the final item clears.

Respect smaller screens

Shorten or disable pinning where vertical space is limited.

Idea 06 · Story panels

Three small states in one continuous move

Opening frame

Set the starting tone before any motion takes over.

Middle state

Shift copy and emphasis as the timeline advances.

Closing note

Resolve into a final, quieter message at the end.

Idea 07 · Masked reveal

An image that resolves as you arrive

Instead of popping into place, the frame quietly uncovers itself in sync with your scroll.

Idea 08 · Metric focus

percent delivery confidence

A single number, carefully lifted

Idea 09 · Horizontal track

Cards that move sideways as you scroll down

A masked lane lets the inner cards glide horizontally while the page itself continues vertically.

First pass

Introduce the concept in a compact tile.

Second frame

Shift the focus without changing the context.

Third moment

End on a slightly slower, more considered card.

Idea 10 · Layered depth

Foreground and background ease into alignment

Stacked cards move at slightly different speeds, creating a quiet sense of depth as you cross the section.

Background layer

A softer, broader surface that barely shifts.

Middle layer

A supporting card that moves slightly more than the back.

Foreground layer

The primary surface, moving the most and holding the focus.
